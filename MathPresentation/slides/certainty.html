Certainty of Mathematics by Nick The Turtle

Mathematics is an extremely certain AoK.
All known mathematical theorem can be derived from basic premises, all of which are based on intuition are are unlikely to be false. However, there are premises which validity has been disputed.
Euclidean geometry includes five axioms:
  • Any two points can be connected via a straight line.
  • Any straight line can be extended by a finite amount.
  • A circle can be described with the position of its center and the length of its radius.
  • All right angles are equal in measure to each other.
  • If a straight line crosses two lines, forming two interior angles which sum is less than 180°, than the two lines will meet on the same side if extended indefinitely.
There are several mathematical theories for which one or more premises (especially the last one) are false, leading to interesting results (two lines perpendicular to a third ultimately intersecting or increasing in distance from each other, the sum of the angles of a triangle not equalling 180°, etc.). According to Einstein's theory of relativity, our universe does not truly follow the above axioms. It is also not known whether our universe on average follows the above axioms.
