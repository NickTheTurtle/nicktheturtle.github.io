Mathematics and WoK by Nick The Turtle

Mathematics uses reason, intuition, and language to gain knowledge.
Reason
Mathematics is built on certain premises derived from common sense. Mathematicians use these premises to discover new knowledge.
For example, geometry normally used (Euclidean geometry) is based on certain axioms. Euclid gave the following five in the Elements:
  • Any two points can be connected via a straight line.
  • Any straight line can be extended by a finite amount.
  • A circle can be described with the position of its center and the length of its radius.
  • All right angles are equal in measure to each other.
  • If a straight line crosses two lines, forming two interior angles which sum is less than 180°, than the two lines will meet on the same side if extended indefinitely.
Intuition
Intuition is constantly used in mathematics, especially when constructing axioms of math. However, intuition cannot always be trusted since math frequently produces counterintuitive results (the Banach-Tarski paradox, Gabriel's Horn, Galileo's paradox, Russell's paradox, the Monty Hall problem, coastline paradox, etc.).
Language
Mathematics has been called the language of science by Galileo.
